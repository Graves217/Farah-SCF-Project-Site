March Update on My SCF Journey

March has been one of the most productive months of my first year at the State College of Florida. Between coursework, hands-on lab sessions, and independent study, I feel genuine momentum building in my understanding of Information Technology. The classroom instruction has been rigorous but rewarding, and each week brings new concepts that push me to think more critically about how technology systems are designed, secured, and maintained. What excites me most is that the material does not feel abstract — every topic connects directly to scenarios I can envision facing in a real IT role. This newsletter is my way of reflecting on that progress and sharing it with anyone who is curious about the path from student to IT professional.

💡 Tip of the Month

Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) everywhere you can. According to recent cybersecurity reports, MFA blocks more than 99% of automated credential-stuffing attacks. Whether you are securing a personal email account or a corporate network resource, adding that second layer of verification is one of the single most impactful steps you can take to protect sensitive data. If your organization has not yet mandated MFA across all user accounts, advocate for it — the cost of implementation is far lower than the cost of a breach.

The field of Information Technology is not simply about understanding hardware and software; it is about understanding people, processes, and the way technology shapes every aspect of modern life. Every time we configure a network, write a line of code, or troubleshoot a system failure, we are contributing to an infrastructure that billions of people depend on daily. This responsibility demands both technical excellence and a commitment to ethical practice. As aspiring IT professionals, we must cultivate not only our technical skills but also our ability to communicate clearly, collaborate effectively, and adapt to change with confidence and agility. The most impactful technologists are those who never stop learning, never stop asking questions, and never underestimate the human dimension of every technical challenge.

Project Spotlight

The centerpiece of my coursework this term has been building this very portfolio website from the ground up using HTML5 and CSS3. The project challenged me to move beyond simply following tutorials and instead make deliberate design decisions: selecting a color palette that communicates professionalism, structuring navigation so that every page is reachable within one click, and writing semantic markup that is both accessible and easy to maintain. Every element on the site — from the rollover navigation links to the responsive layout — represents a skill I had to research, practice, and refine through iteration.

One of the most valuable lessons this project taught me is that web development is as much about communication as it is about code. A page that renders correctly but confuses visitors has failed its purpose. Balancing aesthetics with usability, and creativity with consistency, required me to think like a user rather than just a developer. I am proud of what the site has become, and I plan to continue expanding it as I complete additional courses and accumulate more projects worth showcasing.

  • TCP/IP networking fundamentals and subnetting
  • Cybersecurity principles: CIA triad, threat modeling, and risk assessment

Skills I Am Strengthening

Beyond web design, I have been dedicating significant study time to networking concepts, particularly the TCP/IP model, subnetting, and the role of protocols such as DNS, DHCP, and HTTP in everyday network communication. Understanding how data travels from one endpoint to another — through routers, switches, and firewalls — has given me a much deeper appreciation for the infrastructure that underpins modern digital life. These fundamentals are directly applicable to the CompTIA A+ and Network+ certifications I am targeting, and I can already see how they inform troubleshooting decisions in real support scenarios.

Cybersecurity is another area I am investing in heavily this spring. The CIA triad — Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability — has become a framework I apply instinctively whenever I evaluate a technology decision. I have been working through threat-modeling exercises and studying common attack vectors such as phishing, man-in-the-middle attacks, and social engineering. The more I learn, the more I realize that strong security posture is not a product you purchase; it is a mindset and a discipline that must be built into every layer of an IT environment from the very beginning.

What I Am Exploring in the Tech World

Outside of my assigned coursework, I have been independently exploring the growing intersection of cloud computing and automation. Services such as Microsoft Azure and Amazon Web Services are reshaping how organizations deploy and manage infrastructure, shifting responsibility from on-premises hardware to scalable, pay-as-you-go cloud resources. I have been experimenting with free-tier accounts and following along with documentation tutorials to understand core concepts like virtual machines, storage buckets, and identity and access management (IAM). Seeing the same security principles from my cybersecurity studies applied at cloud scale has been one of the most eye-opening experiences of the semester, and it has solidified my interest in pursuing a cloud-related specialization later in my program.

Looking Ahead

As the spring semester moves into its final stretch, I am focused on finishing strong and positioning myself for the next stage of my IT education. My immediate goal is to complete the CompTIA A+ certification exam before summer, which will serve as a tangible credential that validates the foundational skills I have been building all year. Beyond certification, I am planning to set up a home lab environment where I can practice configuring virtual networks, experimenting with Linux distributions, and simulating common IT support scenarios in a safe, controlled setting. Thank you for following along on this journey — your interest and encouragement mean a great deal. I look forward to sharing even more progress in the next issue, and as always, I welcome your questions and feedback through the Contact page.
